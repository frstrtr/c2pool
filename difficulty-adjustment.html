Block mediantime

The block mediantime is the median of the timestamps of the last 11 blocks. The median is more robust than a single block’s timestamp and mitigates outliers or manipulated times.

Retargeting

Bitcoin recalculates difficulty every 2016 blocks (~two weeks) to hold an average block time near 10 minutes. If the measured timespan over the window exceeds the target, difficulty falls; if it is shorter, difficulty rises.

retargeting_factor = time_diff / (2016 × 10 × 60)
new_target          = current_target × retargeting_factor      (clamped to ×4 / ÷4)
difficulty          = max_target / new_target
V37Target vs. difficulty — keep them distinct.

Difficulty and target are inverses (difficulty = max_target / target). The retarget multiplies the target by the timespan ratio; difficulty then moves the opposite way. Stating it as “new difficulty = current difficulty × factor” inverts the direction — faster blocks (smaller ratio) must raise difficulty, not lower it. The formula above keeps the target on the left and derives difficulty last.

Two forward-looking points from the V37 work:

  • O(1) absolute retarget. Walking the chain to retarget is expensive at large windows. An absolute (ASERT-style) schedule computes the target directly from an anchor in constant time and is immune to time-warp by construction — a candidate for the sharechain’s own difficulty.
  • Time-denominated, adaptive window. V37’s payout window is measured in time (origin-time bins on the underlying-chain clock), not a fixed share count, and widens when the pool’s share of network work is low — set only from exogenous, committed chain state so no participant can inflate it.
